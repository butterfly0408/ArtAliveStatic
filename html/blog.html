Feabruary Teacher Spotlight - Gini Harris

Gini Harris was born in 1964 in Benoni on the East Rand, Gauteng. She matriculated in 1982 and thereafter studied Graphic Design at the Witwatersrand Technikon. She worked as a technical illustrator and at the same time also developed her fine art career completing many successful commissions.

In 2008 Gini discovered miniature art and was instantly hooked. Since then she has had a great deal of success winning many awards and participating in numerous exhibitions both internationally and locally. Most recently in 2021 she received the highly prestigious MAA Award making her a member of the Miniature Artists of America.

She is well known for her commissioned landscapes and portraits. Her favourite subject to paint is people, especially in larger artworks, but also love painting landscapes, plants and trees, still lives and most recently, botanical art in miniature.

Although happy to work to any size and proficient in many styles and mediums, she has had a great deal of success with miniature art in oils. Apart from commissioned artworks, she works mostly from her own photographs, obsessively collecting reference material and often keeping photos for years before painting them.

Gini has worked as a full time artist for more than 35 years and only recently has she taken up the challenge of teaching.

Gini shares her latest works on Facebook and Instagram. If you're captivated by her artistry, consider commissioning a piece. Dive deeper into the world of this remarkable artist, who also shares her knowledge to adults at our school every other Saturday.

I will leave you with a quote by Luke Scott: It’s all art. The way you laugh. Your smile. Your wardrobe & the way you decorate your home. The way you dance. Your energy. Your collection of books & the way you write. Your playlists. Your grocery lists. Your friendships. It’s all art. Your whole life is art.

Art Alive Update: A Colorful February Journey into Pastels

Greetings Art Alive community!

As we step into the vibrant canvas of February, our focus at the Art Alive Art School turns to the enchanting world of pastels. 🎨✨ While our talented students continue to express their creativity on individual canvases, this month offers a special opportunity to delve deeper into the fascinating medium of pastels, drawing inspiration from the masterpieces of both Edgar Degas and the renowned David Johnson.

A warm welcome to the new artists who joined us in January! Our inclusive small classes ensure a friendly atmosphere, and remember, you can join our creative family at any point throughout the year.

Excitement bubbles as we unveil our workshop program for 2024! 🌟 Beginners can explore the magic of oil paints, a wonderfully forgiving medium. Meanwhile, our advanced artists will embark on a journey of discovering new methods, playing with colors, and mastering various tips and techniques.

🎨 Unlock Your Creativity: Upcoming Oil Painting Workshops!

Join us on a captivating artistic journey as we dive into a series of engaging oil painting workshops. Each session promises an opportunity for you to unleash your creativity and craft your own masterpiece. Mark your calendars for these inspiring dates and themes:

2 March: Back to Basics for Beginners
Rediscover the fundamental techniques that form the foundation of captivating artwork.

6 April: Exploring the Importance of Colour
Immerse yourself in the world of color theory and discover how it can elevate your artistic expression.

1 May: Seascapes Unveiled
Capture the beauty of the sea as we guide you through the creation of mesmerizing seascape paintings.

29 June: Tonal Harmony with Warm and Cold Colours
Delve into the nuances of tonal value, exploring the interplay between warm and cold colors for dynamic compositions.

7 September: Perspective Matters
Learn the art of creating depth and dimension in your paintings through the mastery of perspective.

5 October: Palette Knife Mastery
Unleash the unique textures and effects achievable with palette knife techniques, adding a new dimension to your art.

Embark on this creative journey with us, where every stroke tells a story. Whether you're a beginner or seasoned artist, each workshop is crafted to nurture your skills and ignite your passion for art. Don't miss out – secure your spot and let your creativity flourish! 🌟🖌️

February brings a special highlight – our Teacher of the Month, the accomplished Gini Harris. 🌈🖌️ Gini's artistic journey started at a young age, and her studies have only enhanced her innate talent. Spend a moment admiring her outstanding artwork, where passion radiates through every brushstroke. Gini has garnered international acclaim and numerous prizes, showcasing her expertise in themes ranging from buildings and plants to captivating still life and the wonders of nature. Witness the magic as she crafts intricate miniatures with her immense talent.

Join us on this exciting artistic journey at Art Alive, where creativity knows no bounds. Stay tuned for more updates and let's paint a beautiful February together! 🌈🎨✨

Welcome Back

It was with great excitement that we re-opened in January, welcoming back familiar faces, and embracing the fresh energy of new talents. During our recent teacher's meeting, we discussed the exciting roadmap that will guide our artistic learning throughout the year.

In 2024, we're not just teaching art; we're crafting an experience. A journey through diverse art mediums, all while ensuring a steadfast focus on the art elements and principles that form the foundation of every great work of art.

What sets our school apart is the invitation extended to artists throughout the year. No need to wait for a specific enrollment period – the door is always open for those eager to immerse themselves in a world of artistic exploration. Whether you're a seasoned artist or just discovering your passion, our inclusive atmosphere invites you to join at any moment, making art an integral part of your life whenever inspiration strikes.

At the end of January, we will be announcing our Art Workshop Programme. These workshops are open to anyone wanting to learn more about painting.

Picture a cozy ambiance, the aroma of freshly brewed coffee, and a canvas awaiting your touch. In each session, we delve into themed exploration, guiding participants step by step as they create their own masterpieces. In these workshops, you'll paint alongside us, exchanging ideas with fellow artists. Whether you're a seasoned painter or a novice, our workshops offer a welcoming space to nurture your creativity, where every stroke is a learning experience.

Join us in this delightful fusion of art and coffee culture, where the journey is as enriching as the destination, and creativity thrives in the heart of Benoni's coffee scene.

Starting February 1, 2024, keep an eye out for our "Teacher of the Month" feature. This is a tribute to the dedicated Artists who inspire and guide our students towards artistic excellence.

Join us on this journey of artistic exploration, where every stroke and every shade contribute to growing your artistic talent.

News on our workshops and Teacher of the month will be added here and on our social media platforms.

Happy New Year

As we say goodbye to another incredible year at Art Alive Art School, we reflect on the canvases we've filled, the skills we've learned, and the creativity that has flourished.

I am reading a book by Emily P. Freeman, where she talks about uncovering the Art we were made to live and that we should move towards that which makes us feel alive. For me spending time with loved ones and creating my Art always makes me feel alive.

In her book, A million little ways, Emily quotes this from John Acuff: “More often than not, finding out what you love doing most is about recovering an old love or an inescapable truth that has been silenced for years, even decades. When you come to your dream job, your thing, it is rarely a first encounter. It's usually a reunion. So instead of setting out to discover this thing you love doing, you've got to change your thinking and set out to recover it, maybe even rescue it!”

This quote made me think that all of our students should be proud of the reunion that they have made with their art. May we all continue to explore our creativity.

I wish all our talented artists a New Year filled with boundless inspiration, vibrant palettes, and the joy of endless artistic possibilities.

May your brushes dance across the canvas of 2024!

Happy New Year from myself and our wonderful teachers. We look forward to welcoming you back at the School.
